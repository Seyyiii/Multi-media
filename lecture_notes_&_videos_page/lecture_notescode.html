Learn about digital audio, sampling, quantization, and audio formats.Chapter 3: Audio and Video in Multimedia (Short Note)

⃣ Audio in Multimedia Audio refers to sound, music, speech, and any audible information. Types of Audio Analog audio — natural sound (e.g., human voice, instruments) Digital audio — sound converted into binary form for storage and processing Key Terms Sampling: Taking small pieces of the sound wave at regular intervals bit Depth: Determines sound quality (8-bit, 16-bit, 24-bit) Sample Rate: Number of samples per second (e.g., 44.1 kHz) Audio Formats MP3, AAC, WAV, OGG Uses Background music Narration Sound effects Voice commands

2️⃣ Video in Multimedia Video is a sequence of images (frames) shown rapidly to create motion. Key Terms Frame Rate (fps): Frames per second (e.g., 24fps, 30fps, 60fps) Resolution: Clarity of the video (e.g., 1080p, 4K) Compression: Reducing file size using codecs Video Formats MP4, AVI, MOV, MKV Video Codecs H.264, H.265, VP9 Uses Tutorials Advertisements Entertainment Online courses 3️⃣ Importance of Audio & Video Makes learning more interactive Makes presentations more engaging Delivers information faster and more clearly Enhances user experience

4️⃣ Challenges Large file sizes Requires compression Needs high processing power Requires fast internet for streaming
